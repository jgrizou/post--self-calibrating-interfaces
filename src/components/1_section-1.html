The PIN-entering interface

Our interface is designed for user to type a PIN. It is just like when you go to an ATM to get cash out and are asked to type your secret PIN. But here, instead of typing directly on the digits, you have to answer a question from the machine.

The interface (Figure 1) is showing all possible digits (from 0 to 9) each colored in one of two colors (yellow or grey). Think of it as asking you: "What color is your digit?"The current digit considered by the machine is indicated by the dark frame around it. See top-left part of Figure 1.. You look at the digit you want to enter and answer: "My digit is yellow/grey" by clicking on the button with the corresponding color.

For example, in Figure 1, the machine asks you the color of the first digit of your PINIndicated by the dark frame around the left most digit in the top of Figure 1. If you wanted to type a '1', you would click on the grey button because the digit '1' is colored in grey by the machine.

Because the machine decides the colors to apply on each digits, when you click on the grey button, it can immediatly discard half of the digits (all the yellow ones). By repeating the process 3 or 4 times, it can identify with certainty your first digit, and move to the second one.
